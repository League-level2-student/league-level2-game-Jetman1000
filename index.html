Dinosaur Game

by: Connor Bansasine

This game was made for my level 2 project at The League of Amazing Programmers.
Click here to download and play!

1. Why I Made This Game

I decided to make this game to start somewhere in my journey on making and programming games. Although it is pretty bad now, I hope that I could improve on this experience and make even better programs. The reason why I chose the dinosaur game other than anything else is because it is simple game and also a challenge for me. Also, this game is just addicting to play.

2. What I Learned

I learned plenty of tips and advice from user testing. For instance, I needed to make the game a little bit more interesting and exciting to play. I also receive more information and knowledge of coding while making this game and after.

3. Architecture of My Game

There's a dinosaur, and obstacles. The goal is to make the dinosaur not touch the obstacles. There are also different classes for specific things. For example, there is a dinosaur class and obstacle class that are specifically for the dinosaur and obstacles.

4. Technical Implementation

An example of a technical implementation of my game that I am proud is the transitions between the menu, game, and ending state. The way that worked was that I made member variables for all the states of the game, which then assigned them values and their current state. There was the MENU, GAME, and END. In my keyPressed method, every time the ENTER key was pressed, the value increases and transitions to the next. Although this was a simple action, I am very proud of this implementation in my game.

5. Most Challenging Part

Probably the most challenging part of this project was the coding for the dinosaur jumping and the spawning of obstacles. I had to understand what a timer was, velocity, and gravity.

6. Future Fixes and Updates

I'd like to make it cleaner and add additional features that would make it even more addicting and exciting to play.
